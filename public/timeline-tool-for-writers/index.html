Your world's history, in chronological order.

Why writers need a timeline tool

A fictional world exists in time as well as space. Without a clear timeline, stories develop contradictions — a character dies before they were born, a war ends before it begins, a discovery happens twice. A timeline tool prevents all of that.

World History Timeline

Document the ages, eras, and epochs that shaped your world before your story begins. Record wars, the rise and fall of empires, natural catastrophes, magical events, and founding moments. Give your world a past that feels earned.

Story & Character Arcs

Track what happens to your characters over the course of your story. When did they meet? When did they lose someone? When did they change? A character timeline reveals patterns and gaps in their journey.

Campaign Session Log

For dungeon masters, record what happened each session and when it occurred in-world. Build a living chronicle of your campaign that the whole party can reference — and that the world reacts to.

In-World Calendar

Your world doesn't have to run on Gregorian months and seven-day weeks. Track events in your world's own calendar system. Seasons, moons, and ages — defined by your world's logic, not ours.

Chronological clarity

Never let your timeline contradict your story.
